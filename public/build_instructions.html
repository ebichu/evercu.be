1. Preparation

1.1 Fetch necessary tools

Make sure that you have the following tools on hand:

  • large screwdriver (Phillips #2)
  • small screwdriver (Phillips #0)
  • pliers / scissors
  • lighter

1.2 Check kit contents

Unpack the Evercube kit, and check that all parts are there:

  • main components
    • stainless-steel enclosure
    • aluminum disk scaffold
    • SheevaPlug
    • 5-port SATA backplane
    • 140mm fan
    • power supply
  • cables
    • power cable (inside SheevaPlug box)
    • miniUSB-to-USB cable (inside SheevaPlug box)
    • 2m flat ethernet cable
    • 0.5m eSATA-to-SATA cable
  • small parts
    • 5 plastic wedges (brown)
    • 2 Wago power clamps (grey, orange)
    • 2 ScotchLok power clamps (transparent, yellow)
    • 8 nylon washers (white)
    • 4 long screws
    • 4 short screws
    • 4 screw nuts
    • 4 nylon spacers (black)
    • 2 power connectors + end caps (red)
    • 2 wires
    • heat-shrink tube (black)
    • rubber profile (black)
    • adhesive dots (transparent)

If anything should be missing, please contact me.

1.3 Verify that the SheevaPlug is working

Before we go ahead and void its warranty, let's verify that the SheevaPlug boots up, and that we can connect to it over the serial interface.

Plug the SheevaPlug into a power socket and hook it up to your computer via USB. The small end of the included USB cable goes into the miniUSB port on the side of the SheevaPlug. Then follow the instructions on plugcomputer.org (for Windows, Linux & Mac) to connect with a suitable terminal program.

The default login is root with password nosoup4u. Once you have logged in successfully, you can power off the plug again with shutdown -P now.
